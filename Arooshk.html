What Is Calligraphy?

Calligraphy is the art of producing decorative handwriting or lettering with a pen or brush. Calligraphy has been around for about 3,000 years! But I won't be teaching how they did it in the past. Instead I will be teaching you modern calligraphy, the modern twist on calligraphy from the past. Now it is time to really get started!

The Basic Strokes

  • Upstroke:

    • To do the upstroke start at the bottom and move upwards with very little pressure

  • Downstroke:

    • To do a downstroke start at the top and move downwards kind of making a curve with heavy pressure

  • Overturn:

    • The overturn is a combination of both the upstroke and downstroke. For this stroke you are going to start at the bottom moving upwards, applying light pressure this is the upstroke then slightly curve at the top then and applying heavy pressure the downstroke. Make sure the two lines are parallel.

  • Underturn:

    • The underturn is the opposite of the overturn. Start at the top moving downwards with heavy pressure. Then making a small curve transition into a light upstroke. Again make sure both lines are parallel.

  • Compound Curve:

    • A compound curve is a combination of both the overturn and the underturn. For this stroke we are going to start with an overturn and then transition into an underturn. Watch out for sharp corners, we need curves and everything should be in one motion. There should be 3 lines not 4. Make sure they are all parallel.

  • Oval:

    • To do the oval we are going to start at the right side. Start with an upstroke and at the top transition into a downstroke move downwards, then with another transition at the bottom, move back up applying light pressure to complete the oval. Make sure the top and bottom aren't pointy, but curved.

  • Ascending Loop:

    • For this stroke you'll start in the middle with a light upstroke. You will make half of a loop and when you reach the top you will make a slight curve and transition into a heavy downstroke.

  • Descending Loop:

    • The descending loop is the opposite of the ascending loop. You will start at the top and move downwards with a heavy downstroke. At the bottom make a slight curve and transition into a light upstroke making a loop.
